Panagbenga Festival is an annual flower festival in Baguio City, Philippines, celebrated throughout February. The term "Panagbenga" means "season of blooming" in the Kankanaey language, symbolizing the city's recovery and resilience after the 1990 Luzon earthquake. It showcases the vibrant culture of the Cordillera region through spectacular parades, floral displays, and dynamic performances.

The Panagbenga Festival was first held in 1996 as a way to boost tourism and the local economy after the devastation caused by the 1990 earthquake. It was conceptualized by Atty. Damaso Bangaoet Jr. and supported by the Baguio Flower Festival Foundation, Inc. The festival has since evolved into a grand celebration that highlights the beauty of Baguio and its indigenous traditions.
